What is Incences?

In answer to the question of what is incense, the answer can be given that the word incense means an object that produces smoke in old Turkish. Incense, which comes from the old Turkish as the origin of the name; It is a material that emits various odors when burned and is used to suppress bad odors or to emit energy and clear the energy of the person or the environment. Although incense can be of different types, the function of each incense is different. Incenses, which reveal odors according to their types when burned, help to increase focus as well as open the mind. For this reason, incense can be burned while performing activities that require focus, such as yoga, meditation, and reiki. In addition, it can be used before or after rituals, i.e. rituals, or during rituals in different religions and different cultures.

Prayer, magic and amulets were used in the treatment processes. Pre-Islamic Arab societies also applied to soothsayers and sorcerers for their treatment. Burning incense can be considered as one of these treatment methods. These soothsayers, who burned incense while praying or doing magic, formed the basis of the use of incense. The area of ​​use of incense, which started to be used in religious rites, expanded as time passed, and thanks to changing cultures, lifestyles and religious beliefs, it began to be used in daily life and spread its use in religious architectures.
